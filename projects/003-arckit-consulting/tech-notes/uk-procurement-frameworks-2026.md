## Summary

A consolidated reference of the four major UK Government procurement frameworks (G-Cloud, DOS, MCF, and direct award routes) as of 2026-05-07, including current iteration, application status, lot structure, SME standing, fees, and lead times. This is the consulting-practice-side perspective; the buyer-side perspective is covered by the related guidance issued by Crown Commercial Service.

## Key Findings

### G-Cloud (Cloud Software, Cloud Hosting, Cloud Support)

- **Current iteration**: G-Cloud 14 (RM1557.14) live, originally 18-month term ending April 2026, **extended for 6 months until October 2026**.
- **G-Cloud 15**: applications closed January 2026; expected go-live in 2026.
- **G-Cloud 16**: pending CCS announcement; next available application window for new entrants.
- **Lots**: 1 (Cloud Hosting), 2 (Cloud Software), 3 (Cloud Support — most relevant for EA / consulting), 4 (Cloud Support — Further Competition Only).
- **Framework fee**: 1% of revenue invoiced through G-Cloud.
- **Pricing constraint**: service offers and pricing must remain fixed for the framework duration; only price reductions allowed.
- **Application bar**: economic and financial standing assessments introduced for G-Cloud 14 onwards (Lots 1–3).
- **Catalogue scale**: 4,000+ suppliers, 46,000+ services on G-Cloud 14.
- **SME standing**: framework is uncapped (any compliant application joins); CCS publishes SME spend data as a positive case study.

### Digital Outcomes and Specialists (DOS 6, DOS 7)

- **DOS 6 (RM1043.8)**: live until 27 June 2026 (extended from earlier expiry).
- **DOS 7 (RM1043.9)**: live since 1 April 2026; replaces DOS 6.
- **DOS 8 / DSP successor**: pending CCS announcement; next open window likely 2027 onwards.
- **Routes**: Digital Outcomes (bespoke teams) and Digital Specialists (individual roles, day-rate).
- **SME share**: historically c. 33% of £6 billion+ DOS spend was with SME providers.
- **Day-rate caps**: published per specialist role, varying by role and (sometimes) region.
- **Bid mechanism**: buyers post requests; eligible suppliers respond with CV / specialist profile + rate.

### Management Consultancy Framework (MCF3, MCF4)

- **MCF3 (RM6187)**: superseded by MCF4 in July 2025.
- **MCF4 (RM6309)**: live 29 July 2025; expires 28 July 2027.
- **MCF5**: pending; expected 2027 announcement.
- **Lots (MCF4)**: 10 lots from Business (Lot 1) through Restructuring and Insolvency (Lot 10). EA-relevant lots: 2 (Strategy and Policy), 3 (Complex and Transformation), 8 (Infrastructure).
- **Framework duration**: 2 years (typical for MCF iterations).
- **Direct prime entry as a new SME**: effectively impossible; sub-contracting under existing primes is the realistic v1 route.
- **Spend controls (2026)**: ministerial sign-off for any consultancy spend over £600,000 or contracts over 9 months; permanent secretary sign-off for spend over £100,000 lasting more than 3 months.

### Direct Award (Under Threshold)

- **Threshold (Procurement Act 2023, January 2026)**: £213,477 ex VAT central government services; £329,083 ex VAT sub-central / NHS / education / local authorities.
- **Below threshold**: contracting authority's internal procurement rules apply (typically 3-quote competition or single supplier under delegated authority limit).
- **Best fit for**: SME-tier engagements (typically well below threshold), discovery / readiness packages, repeat business (subject to CoI controls).

### Prompt Payment (PPN compliance)

- **Section 73(1) Procurement Act 2023** implies 30-day payment terms into every public sub-contract.
- **UK Gov target**: 90% of undisputed and valid SME invoices paid within 5 days; 100% within 30 days.
- **PPN 021 payment spot checks**: contracting authorities can audit prime contractors' payment performance to sub-contractors.
