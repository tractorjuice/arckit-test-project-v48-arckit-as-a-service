## Summary

Reference of the four primary UK consulting pricing models — day-rate (T&M), fixed-price, retainer / advisory, and outcome-based — with 2026 market reference points and UK Government policy guidance. Particular emphasis on the explicit UK Government warning against inappropriate outcome-based pricing where outcomes are beyond the supplier's control.

### Retainer / Advisory Reference Points

- Junior advisory retainer: £8,000–£12,000/month (6–10 days/month).
- Standard EA advisory retainer: £12,000–£20,000/month (10–18 days/month).
- Senior strategic advisory retainer: £20,000–£40,000/month (Practice Lead + Senior, board-facing).
- Minimum commitment: typically 3, 6, or 12 months.
- Quota management: caps on meetings, response time, deliverables, change requests.

### Outcome-Based Pricing — UK Government Warning

UK Government's Sourcing and Consultancy Playbooks **explicitly warn against inappropriate use of outcome-based pricing**:

> "in several high-profile contracts, risk transfer has been inappropriately transferred through the pricing mechanism where suppliers were inappropriately paid on outcomes, with payment linked to factors beyond their control and leaving suppliers exposed to the risk of not being paid for their services where desired outcomes were not achieved."

The Risk Allocation and Pricing Approaches Guidance recommends "risk pots" — explicitly priced and visible — rather than implicit risk transfer through outcome pricing.

### Hybrid Pricing Strategy (Recommended)

Combine: day-rate for specialist roles, fixed-price for defined packages, retainer for ongoing advisory, SME-tier pro-rata for verified SMEs. Avoid outcome-based / risk-share pricing in v1.
