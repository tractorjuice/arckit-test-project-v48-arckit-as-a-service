## Executive Summary

### Purpose

This document identifies the key stakeholders for **ArcKit Consulting** — the professional-services arm that uses the ArcKit toolkit to deliver enterprise architecture engagements for the UK Public Sector and the SMEs that supply it — surfaces the underlying drivers that motivate them, traces those drivers into measurable goals, and maps the goals to business outcomes that prove satisfaction.

The analysis is the foundation for prioritising requirements (`ARC-003-REQ-v1.0.md`), staging the SOBC (`/arckit:sobc`), framing the risk register (`/arckit:risk`), and designing the engagement and communication plan for practice operations.

### Key Findings

The practice has an unusually **principled** stakeholder set: the most senior internal stakeholder (Practice Lead / SRO) is materially constrained by an externally-published commitment (Principle 1, ARC-000-PRIN-v2.0 — SME affordability and cross-subsidy). That alignment is a *strength* — it tilts every internal trade-off in favour of public-value behaviour — but it is also the **single largest area of latent conflict**: SME affordability vs. sustainable margin, open knowledge contribution vs. competitive advantage, prompt-payment / framework discipline vs. operational expedience. The dominant external stakeholders (CCS framework managers, GDS/CDDO assurance bodies, ICO, HMT) all reinforce the same direction of travel — which is rare and useful — provided the practice respects framework conduct, evidence-grade quality, and information-governance discipline.

Two stakeholder groups warrant disproportionate engagement effort: **Client SROs** (gov department buyers; high power, high interest, career-sensitive to NAO/PAC scrutiny) and **the ArcKit Architecture Review Board** (internal governance with veto over any practice behaviour that contradicts the principles). Two groups warrant disproportionate listening effort: **Consultants and Associates** (the deliverable; high churn risk if not engaged) and **SME Founders** (the mission's beneficiaries; their voice is often absent from formal governance).

### Critical Success Factors

- **Principle 1 honoured operationally**: SME-tier capacity reservation published, delivered, and externally verifiable; cross-subsidy ≥ 10% net margin paid annually.
- **Quality discipline non-negotiable**: 100% of client deliverables pass the ArcKit quality checklist; zero independent-assurance failures attributable to the practice.
- **Information governance proven**: Cyber Essentials Plus continuous; zero cross-engagement data leakage; UK GDPR compliance evidenced.
- **Framework standing live**: G-Cloud and DOS listings active without lapse; CCS framework conduct compliant.
- **Healthy pipeline and balanced concentration**: Pipeline coverage ≥ 3.0; no single client > 30% of forward 90-day revenue.
- **Brand integrity preserved**: 0 publicised quality or confidentiality incidents; client NPS ≥ +40 sustained.

### Stakeholder Alignment Score

**Overall Alignment**: HIGH (with named, manageable conflicts)

The internal stakeholder set is unusually well-aligned because the practice was conceived around the same principles set (`ARC-000-PRIN-v2.0.md`) that governs the product projects. The major external stakeholders (CCS, GDS/CDDO, ICO, HMT, NAO scrutiny posture) all reinforce the same direction. The principal conflicts are **internal trade-offs** between commercial pressure (Engagement Director, Commercial Lead) and principled discipline (Practice Lead, QA Lead, DPO, ARB). They are documented honestly below with named resolutions and decision authorities.

## Stakeholder Drivers Analysis

### SD-1: Practice Lead / SRO (Mark Craddock) — Build a principled, sustainable practice

**Stakeholder**: Practice Lead / Senior Responsible Owner.

**Driver Category**: STRATEGIC + PERSONAL + RISK.

**Driver Statement**: Establish ArcKit Consulting as a principled, financially-sustainable UK Public Sector consulting practice that *demonstrably honours* the ArcKit principles (especially Principle 1 SME affordability and Principle 16 Open Source / Reuse) while protecting the ArcKit brand across the product / sovereign / consulting trio.

**Context & Background**:

The Practice Lead conceived the ArcKit ecosystem and is personally invested in its principles. Principle 1 ("free or substantially cheaper for SMEs") is publicly stated and externally verifiable — failure to honour it operationally would unwind the moral and reputational foundation of the entire ecosystem, not just the consulting practice. Equally, a financially-failing practice cannot fund the cross-subsidy that makes Principle 1 sustainable. The driver therefore *combines* mission and commercial discipline.

**Driver Intensity**: CRITICAL.

**Enablers**:

- Structured cross-subsidy mechanism (BR-004, BC-2 of REQ-003) baked into operations, not optional.
- Strong QA discipline (FR-008, NFR-Q-001) protects brand at the engagement level.
- Public framework standing (G-Cloud, DOS) reduces sales friction and exposes the practice to disciplined buyer expectations.
- Engagement-derived product feedback (BR-010) reinforces the dual-channel strategy.

**Blockers**:

- Pipeline thinness in the early months forcing below-floor pricing.
- Quality failure on a high-profile engagement.
- Cross-engagement confidentiality incident.
- Senior consultant turnover in the first 12 months.
- Framework listing rejection or lapse.

**Related Stakeholders**: Aligned with QA Lead, DPO, ARB, GDS/CDDO. In tension with Engagement Director (utilisation pressure) and Commercial Lead (margin discipline vs win rate).

### SD-2: Engagement Director — Run delivery without fires

**Stakeholder**: Practice Operations / Engagement Director.

**Driver Category**: OPERATIONAL + PERSONAL.

**Driver Statement**: Deliver concurrent client engagements on time, on scope, on quality, and at consultant-utilisation levels that justify the bench cost; protect the delivery team from scope creep; produce a steady stream of well-handed-over engagements without "fires".

**Context & Background**:

In a consulting practice, the Engagement Director is the fulcrum of operational risk. Their KPIs (utilisation %, client satisfaction, milestone slip rate, scope-change discipline) are personal as well as commercial. They feel daily pressure from clients (more scope, faster), from consultants (better utilisation balance, capability time), from Commercial (more bids, faster proposals), and from QA (slow down, do it properly). Their incentives sometimes pull against the Practice Lead's principles when expedience and discipline diverge.

**Driver Intensity**: HIGH.

**Enablers**:

- Strong engagement scoping at bid time (FR-002 templates) reduces mid-engagement firefighting.
- Quality gates (FR-008) catch issues early when they are cheap to fix.
- Reliable cover plans (NFR-A-001) prevent illness / leave from blowing milestones.
- Pattern library (FR-012) reduces re-invention per engagement.

**Blockers**:

- Below-floor or under-scoped bids forced through commercial pressure.
- Consultant churn mid-engagement.
- Client SROs who treat fixed-price engagements as time-and-materials.
- Sub-contractor unreliability.
- Quality gate slipping under deadline pressure.

**Related Stakeholders**: Reinforces Senior Consultants and Client SROs. Tension with Commercial Lead (bid pace), QA Lead (gate vs deadline), Practice Lead (principle vs expedience).

### SD-3: Commercial Lead — Win the right work at the right margin

**Stakeholder**: Commercial / Bid / Framework Lead.

**Driver Category**: FINANCIAL + STRATEGIC.

**Driver Statement**: Build and convert a qualified pipeline; win bids at or above the pricing floor; maintain framework standing without lapse; keep the practice's commercial admin overhead proportionate to the engagement portfolio.

**Context & Background**:

Public-sector EA work is heavily framework-mediated; failing to be on the right frameworks at the right time is fatal to pipeline. CCS framework cycles, MI returns, prompt-payment compliance, PPN obligations all sit with Commercial. Bid effort is high — proposals require capability evidence, named consultants, transparent pricing — and unwinnable bids destroy capacity. The Commercial Lead is the practice's commercial discipline; they feel direct daily tension with Engagement Director (capacity reservation) and with Practice Lead (price floor vs win rate when pipeline is thin).

**Driver Intensity**: HIGH.

**Enablers**:

- Template-driven proposals (FR-002) reduce per-bid effort.
- Framework listings (BR-002) live and current.
- CRM / pipeline tooling (FR-020) gives forward visibility.
- Concentration thresholds (BR-009) discipline portfolio composition.

**Blockers**:

- Pricing-floor enforcement when pipeline is thin (Practice Lead exception path).
- Framework refresh cycles requiring substantial recertification effort.
- Conflict-of-interest disqualifications on attractive opportunities.
- Late-paying clients undermining cash position.

**Related Stakeholders**: Tension with Practice Lead (pricing discipline) and QA Lead (proposal quality vs speed). Aligned with CCS framework managers (compliance is the hygiene factor).

### SD-4: QA Lead — Zero brand-damaging quality failures

**Stakeholder**: Quality Assurance & Peer Review Lead.

**Driver Category**: COMPLIANCE + RISK + STRATEGIC.

**Driver Statement**: Ensure every client deliverable passes the ArcKit quality checklist before sign-off; ensure the practice's internal QA discipline is unmistakable to clients and assurance bodies; protect the brand by keeping a deliverable that cannot withstand independent assurance from ever leaving the practice.

**Context & Background**:

The brand depends on evidence-grade output. A single highly-public failed assurance event (GDS Service Assessment, NCSC review, NAO criticism) attributable to ArcKit Consulting work would unwind years of brand-building and damage the SaaS / sovereign products by association. The QA Lead is therefore in a structurally awkward position: they hold the quality line against deadline pressure, sometimes against the Engagement Director and Commercial Lead. Their authority depends on the Practice Lead backing them every time they hold the line.

**Driver Intensity**: CRITICAL.

**Enablers**:

- Independent reviewer pool sized to demand (FR-008).
- ArcKit quality checklist embedded in tooling (Project 001 / 002).
- Practice Lead escalation path that backs QA against deadline pressure.
- Pattern library (FR-012) reduces fresh-error rate.

**Blockers**:

- Deadline pressure compressing QA gate cycle time below NFR-P-002.
- Independent reviewer scarcity (concentration on too few seniors).
- Cultural drift towards "QA as a rubber stamp" — must be actively resisted.

**Related Stakeholders**: Aligned with Practice Lead, DPO, ArcKit ARB, GDS/CDDO, and Client SROs (who want assurance pass-through). Tension with Engagement Director and Commercial Lead under deadline pressure.

### SD-5: Talent Lead — Build and keep the bench

**Stakeholder**: Talent / Recruitment / Capability Lead.

**Driver Category**: OPERATIONAL + COMPLIANCE.

**Driver Statement**: Recruit, contract, and retain consultants at the right capability mix; manage SC clearance pipelines; maintain IR35 compliance; ensure DEI and equality-of-opportunity in recruitment and engagement allocation; keep churn low.

**Context & Background**:

The deliverable *is* the bench. Senior EA talent in the UK public sector is in short supply and competitively priced. Clearance lead times (BPSS days, SC 3–9 months) create a structural delay between hiring and engaging on sensitive work. IR35 status determinations on associate engagements carry HMRC liability. DEI is both a moral and a Procurement-Act-conduct issue. Churn within the first 12 months is the most damaging operational risk after a quality failure.

**Driver Intensity**: HIGH.

**Enablers**:

- Capability matrix (FR-005) makes recruitment gaps explicit.
- Capability development time (NFR-M-003) reduces churn.
- Clearance pipeline started in advance of demand (NFR-SEC-003).
- Prompt payment to associates (FR-010, NFR-C-002 alignment).

**Blockers**:

- Slow SC clearance pipelines blocking engagement allocation.
- IR35 misclassification risk on associate panel.
- Burnout risk if utilisation targets exceed NFR-S-002 sustainably.
- DEI-thin recruitment pools requiring deliberate outreach effort.

**Related Stakeholders**: Aligned with Senior Consultants, Associate Panel. Tension with Engagement Director under utilisation pressure.

### SD-6: DPO / Information Governance Lead — Provable confidentiality and zero breaches

**Stakeholder**: Data Protection Officer / Information Governance Lead.

**Driver Category**: COMPLIANCE + RISK.

**Driver Statement**: Maintain UK GDPR compliance, ICO good standing, Cyber Essentials Plus continuous certification, zero cross-engagement leakage, and provable incident-response capability; protect the practice (and the DPO personally) from ICO action and the brand from breach publicity.

**Context & Background**:

Confidentiality is the single largest brand-and-contractual risk for any consulting practice handling government data. DPOs have personal statutory standing under UK GDPR / DPA 2018 — their role is partly independent from the practice's commercial pressures. Cross-engagement contamination (accidental or deliberate) would damage every active engagement simultaneously. Departing-consultant exfiltration is a recognised industry pattern. The DPO drives the controls in NFR-SEC-001 to NFR-SEC-007 and FR-017.

**Driver Intensity**: CRITICAL.

**Enablers**:

- Engagement workspace isolation (FR-004) verified by quarterly tests.
- Cyber Essentials Plus continuous certification.
- Annual independent pen test of internal systems.
- Tested incident-response runbook (FR-017) with 72h ICO notification path.
- Departing-consultant control (NFR-SEC-007) operated within 1 working day.

**Blockers**:

- Shadow-IT — consultants storing client material outside sanctioned tooling.
- BYOD pressure for cost savings (prohibited in NFR-SEC-006 for client data).
- Sub-contractor systems that fall outside the practice's certification boundary.
- Pattern-library reuse paths that bypass anonymisation review.

**Related Stakeholders**: Aligned with QA Lead, Practice Lead, Client SROs, ICO, GDS/CDDO. Tension with Engagement Director / Commercial Lead under speed-of-delivery pressure.

### SD-7: Senior Consultants (Principal / Senior level) — Meaningful work, fair utilisation, capability growth

**Stakeholder**: Senior Consultants (employees).

**Driver Category**: PERSONAL + OPERATIONAL.

**Driver Statement**: Deliver work the consultant is professionally proud of; maintain reasonable utilisation (not chronic overload, not chronic bench); develop capability in current and emerging EA practice; have a clear progression path; be paid fairly and on time.

**Context & Background**:

UK public-sector EA capability is competitively priced and in short supply. Senior consultants leave practices that overwork them, that ship work they are not proud of, that under-invest in their capability, or that pay slowly. Retention is partly hygiene (utilisation, pay, devices, tooling) and partly culture (QA discipline, peer-review respect, public-value mission alignment). Consultants are also one of the practice's strongest brand channels — they speak at conferences, write public posts, and shape the public reputation.

**Driver Intensity**: HIGH.

**Enablers**:

- Utilisation in the 65–80% range (NFR-S-002), not chronically above.
- ≥ 5% capability development time (NFR-M-003).
- Quality discipline (NFR-Q-001) — work consultants are proud of.
- Capability matrix (FR-005) supporting their development.
- Public-mission framing (Principle 1).

**Blockers**:

- Chronic over-utilisation > 80%.
- Below-quality deliverables forced through under deadline pressure.
- Slow peer-review cycles.
- Tooling friction (multiple non-SSO logins).
- Slow expense or pay processes.

**Related Stakeholders**: Aligned with Talent Lead and QA Lead. Tension with Engagement Director under utilisation pressure.

### SD-8: Associate Panel / Sub-contractors — Predictable work, prompt payment, fair scope

**Stakeholder**: Associate consultants and sub-contractor delivery firms.

**Driver Category**: FINANCIAL + OPERATIONAL.

**Driver Statement**: Receive a predictable stream of clearly-scoped work, paid promptly within agreed terms, on contracts that respect their independence and IP boundaries.

**Context & Background**:

Associates are typically small businesses or sole-traders whose cash position is sensitive to payment terms; PPN 03/23 (prompt payment) sets a 30-day standard for the public sector and the practice should mirror it for associates. Reputation among associates compounds: a practice that pays slowly or scopes ambiguously will struggle to engage capable associates over time. Associate engagements also carry IR35 liability and sub-contractor security responsibility.

**Driver Intensity**: MEDIUM (rises sharply during cash-flow stress).

**Enablers**:

- Prompt-payment policy mirroring PPN 03/23 (≤ 30 days, FR-010).
- Clear scoping at engagement start.
- Reasonable IP terms in associate agreements (FR-010).
- Predictable workload pipeline.

**Blockers**:

- Late-paying clients forcing the practice to delay associates.
- Mid-engagement scope drift between prime client and associate work.
- IR35 mis-determinations exposing associate to HMRC dispute.

**Related Stakeholders**: Aligned with Engagement Director (for capacity flex) and Talent Lead. Tension with Commercial Lead in cash-flow-stress scenarios.

### SD-9: ArcKit Product Owners (Project 001 SaaS, Project 002 Sovereign) — Real-engagement product feedback and brand reinforcement

**Stakeholder**: ArcKit Product Owners — Project 001 (managed SaaS) and Project 002 (sovereign).

**Driver Category**: STRATEGIC + OPERATIONAL.

**Driver Statement**: Receive structured, anonymised, actionable product feedback from real client engagements that the consulting practice runs; ensure that the consulting practice's conduct does not damage product brand; benefit from joint case studies and reuse contributions.

**Context & Background**:

The ArcKit ecosystem is a deliberate dual-channel strategy: a product line that benefits from a consulting practice that uses it. The strategic asset is the *feedback loop*. Squandering it — by not capturing engagement learning or by not relaying it — wastes the ecosystem's main competitive advantage. Equally, a poorly-run consulting practice damages the product line by association.

**Driver Intensity**: HIGH.

**Enablers**:

- Quarterly structured product-feedback meeting (BR-010).
- Anonymisation pipeline (FR-013) preserving client confidentiality.
- Shared roadmap visibility.

**Blockers**:

- Consulting practice quality failure damaging product brand.
- Feedback captured but never acted on (one-way feedback kills the loop).
- Engagement-derived patterns that conflict with product roadmap direction.

**Related Stakeholders**: Aligned with Practice Lead, ARB. Tension with engagement-confidentiality (mediated by anonymisation).

### SD-10: ArcKit Architecture Review Board (ARB) — Cross-project consistency and principle adherence

**Stakeholder**: ArcKit Architecture Review Board (governance body across Projects 000 / 001 / 002 / 003).

**Driver Category**: COMPLIANCE + STRATEGIC.

**Driver Statement**: Ensure cross-project consistency with the architecture principles (`ARC-000-PRIN-v2.0.md`); approve exceptions formally; protect the integrity of Principle 1 (SME affordability) operationally; ensure the practice does not commercially behave in ways that undermine the principles.

**Context & Background**:

The ARB is a structural check on commercial drift away from the principles. It has decision authority on exception requests and reviews ongoing compliance quarterly. For ArcKit Consulting specifically, the ARB cares most about (a) Principle 1 — that the SME tier is honoured operationally and not eroded under commercial pressure; (b) Principle 5 / 7 / 8 — that confidentiality controls do not slip; (c) Principle 16 — that reuse and open-source contribution happen in practice, not in policy alone.

**Driver Intensity**: HIGH.

**Enablers**:

- Documented quarterly review of principle adherence.
- Annual published Principle-1 affordability validation.
- Transparent exception process when circumstances genuinely require it.

**Blockers**:

### Conflict Analysis

**Competing Drivers**:

- **Conflict C-1**: SD-1 (Practice Lead — SME affordability) vs. SD-3 (Commercial Lead — sustainable margin and pricing-floor discipline).
  - **Nature**: Pure pro-bono SME work has zero or negative gross margin. Excessive SME-tier capacity erodes the margin needed to fund the cross-subsidy and pay competitive consultant rates.
  - **Resolution Strategy** (per REQ-003 C-1): COMPROMISE — published quarterly SME-tier capacity reservation backed by cross-subsidy; additional SME demand met at a published SME pricing floor that remains margin-positive. ARB validates annually.
  - **Decision Authority**: Practice Lead, with ARB oversight on annual affordability review.

- **Conflict C-2**: SD-2 (Engagement Director — utilisation, deadlines) vs. SD-4 (QA Lead — checklist discipline) vs. SD-7 (Senior Consultants — capability development time).
  - **Nature**: High utilisation pressure compresses QA gate cycles and crowds out capability-development time, which degrades quality and increases churn.
  - **Resolution Strategy**: NFR-S-002 utilisation cap at 80% sustained (chronic > 80% triggers leadership review); NFR-M-003 protected ≥ 5% capability-development time; QA gate cycle protected by NFR-P-002. Practice Lead backs QA when deadline pressure rises (SD-4 enabler).
  - **Decision Authority**: Practice Lead.

- **Conflict C-3**: SD-1 (Practice Lead — IP reuse and open contribution) vs. SD-6 (DPO — confidentiality) vs. SD-12 (Client SROs — discretion about their work).
  - **Nature**: Reuse of engagement-derived IP risks leaking client-specific material; case-study publication risks identifying clients without consent.
  - **Resolution Strategy** (per REQ-003 C-3): INNOVATE — anonymised pattern library (FR-012) is the only authorised reuse channel between engagements; no copy-paste from source workspaces; FR-013 publication requires explicit client consent and DPO sign-off.
  - **Decision Authority**: DPO with Practice Lead.

- **Conflict C-4**: SD-3 (Commercial Lead — win rate, framework eligibility on attractive opportunities) vs. SD-1 (Practice Lead — pricing floor) and SD-4 (QA Lead — proposal quality / accuracy).
  - **Nature**: Thin-pipeline pressure tempts below-floor pricing or rushed proposals; both undermine practice integrity.
  - **Resolution Strategy**: BR-004 pricing floor with documented Practice Lead exception path (no silent below-floor bids); FR-002 template-driven proposals reducing time-to-quality conflict; pipeline-coverage discipline (G-9) reducing thin-pipeline desperation.
  - **Decision Authority**: Practice Lead.

- **Conflict C-5**: SD-3 (Commercial Lead — sub-contractor flex for surge capacity) vs. SD-5 (Talent — onboarding rigour) and SD-6 (DPO — security boundary).
  - **Nature**: Surge demand pressure tempts shortcuts in associate onboarding (NDA, IR35, IG training, security boundary verification).
  - **Resolution Strategy**: FR-010 mandatory associate onboarding with same QA standards as employees; capability matrix entries required before allocation; engagement-allocation block until onboarding complete (FR-005, FR-006).
  - **Decision Authority**: Talent Lead with DPO.

- **Conflict C-6**: SD-1 (Practice Lead — Principle 16 open contribution) vs. unstated competitor-paranoia driver in some commercial advisers.
  - **Nature**: Concern that publishing patterns weakens competitive moat.
  - **Resolution Strategy** (per REQ-003 C-5): PRIORITIZE openness. ArcKit's competitive position is built on delivery quality, brand, and ecosystem — not hoarded patterns. Per Principle 16 mandatory.
  - **Decision Authority**: ARB.

- **Conflict C-7**: SD-2 (Engagement Director — engagement velocity) vs. SD-13 (Client Architects — co-design pace) vs. SD-12 (Client SRO — outcome timeline).
  - **Nature**: Co-design rate-limits engagement velocity. SROs sometimes want output speed beyond the counterpart team's absorption rate.
  - **Resolution Strategy**: Co-design pace agreed at kick-off (UC-2 step 1); milestone re-baselining via change request if SRO accelerates beyond counterpart capacity.
  - **Decision Authority**: Engagement Director with Client SRO.

- **Conflict C-8**: SD-12 (Client SRO — discretion / non-publication) vs. SD-1 (Practice Lead — case-study publication) vs. SD-18 (public-scrutiny posture — transparency).
  - **Nature**: Some clients are reluctant to consent to case-study publication even when fully anonymised.
  - **Resolution Strategy**: Client consent normalised at engagement closure (FR-014); fallback to fully-anonymised pattern publication (no client identifier) where case-study consent withheld; volume target (G-12) is across the engagement portfolio, not per engagement.
  - **Decision Authority**: Client SRO has veto on their engagement; Practice Lead manages portfolio volume.
