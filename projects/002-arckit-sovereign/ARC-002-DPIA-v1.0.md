## 1. DPIA Type and Scope

This DPIA addresses the **vendor-side processing** that occurs in a sovereign engagement. The bulk of personal-data processing in a sovereign deployment is **customer-side**, since the deployment runs entirely within the customer boundary. The customer's own DPIA (if applicable) covers customer-side processing; this document records the **interface** between the vendor and the customer for personal-data purposes.

**Vendor-side processing in scope**:

- Receipt and triage of vulnerability disclosures (project 002 INT-010).
- Optional vendor remote support channel content (project 002 INT-009) — opt-in by customer.
- Customer billing contact details (vendor controller for vendor invoicing).
- Operator metadata for cleared-personnel-only support engagements.

**Out of scope** (customer's DPIA): all tenant content; all customer staff personal data inside the deployment; audit logs (customer-controlled per FR-010).

## 3. Risk Assessment (Vendor-Side Only)

| Risk | Vendor mitigation | Residual |
|------|-------------------|----------|
| Vendor staff handling customer personal data without authorisation | BPSS vetting; access logging; need-to-know policy | Low |
| Vulnerability disclosure content reveals exploitable issue without remediation channel | Triage SLAs; secure intake (INT-010) | Low |
| Vendor remote support session captures unintended personal data | Opt-in only; signed; customer-controlled session; audit; auto-redact transcripts where possible | Low |
| Customer billing data leaks via vendor systems | Vendor processor of payment processor; minimal data retained | Low |
