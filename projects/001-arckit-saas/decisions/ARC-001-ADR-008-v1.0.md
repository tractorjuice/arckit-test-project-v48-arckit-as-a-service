## 3. Context and Problem Statement

### 3.1 Problem Description

ADR-001 (tenant_id propagation) prevents one tenant from *seeing* another tenant's data. ADR-006 (managed Kubernetes per cell with namespace quotas) prevents one tenant from *exhausting* an entire cell at the runtime layer. Neither, on its own, prevents a *single* tenant from:

- Hammering the public API hard enough to degrade other tenants in the same cell (NFR-A-003 noisy-neighbour).
- Driving AI inference costs (FR-004, INT-005) past any reasonable cross-subsidy assumption (BR-005, Principle 17).
- Triggering large-tenant exports (FR-006, ADR-007) repeatedly enough that the export pipeline becomes a back-pressure source.
- Filling cell storage faster than NFR-S-002 budgets anticipate.

The platform also needs to differentiate paid tiers (FR-011) — a free SME tenant and a large-enterprise tenant cannot have identical quotas if cross-subsidy economics (BR-005) are to work. And in sovereign mode (project 002), the deploying authority must be able to configure these quotas to match their own internal capacity model rather than inheriting SaaS-shaped defaults.

**Problem statement as a question**: What per-tenant quota, rate-limit, AI-inference budget, export-rate-limit, and fair-use policy should ArcKit operate, such that:

- shared cells are protected from noisy tenants (NFR-A-003 bulkhead),
- per-tenant cost-to-serve is bounded predictably enough for the SME free tier to remain economically viable (BR-001, BR-005, Principle 17),
- paid tiers are commercially differentiable (FR-011),
- the policy is transparent to tenants and recognisable to UK Government assurance,
- and the same controls are configurable by sovereign deploying authorities (project 002)?

### 3.2 Why This Decision Is Needed

- **Business context**: BR-001 (free-tier viability requires a per-tenant cost ceiling), BR-002 (transparent published fair-use), BR-005 (cross-subsidy unit economics), BR-007 (export must be a guaranteed right, not a DDoS vector).
- **Technical context**: NFR-A-003 (bulkhead, graceful degradation), NFR-S-001 (5 000-tenant scale per cell-fill discipline of ADR-001 + ADR-006), NFR-S-002 (50 GB / 1 000 tenants storage envelope), NFR-P-001 (interactive p95 < 3 s), NFR-P-002 (AI generation latency budget), NFR-P-003 (bulk export < 10 min), FR-004 (AI generation), FR-006 (export), FR-008 (public API), FR-011 (billing tiers — quota configuration is the technical surface tier-differentiation rides on), FR-014 (admin console — operator quota override).
- **Regulatory context**: GDS Service Standard Point 5 (everyone can use the service — quotas must not silently lock genuine SME users out), Point 14 (operate a reliable service — fair-use is the discipline that *makes* the service reliable for everyone); UK GDPR fairness principle (transparent processing of usage data); Cyber Essentials (denial-of-service mitigation); Technology Code of Practice Point 4 (security integral — rate-limit is a baseline DoS control).

### 3.3 Supporting Links

- **Requirements**: BR-001/002/005/007; FR-004, FR-006, FR-008, FR-011, FR-014; NFR-A-003, NFR-S-001/002, NFR-P-001/002/003.
- **Related ADRs**: ADR-001 (parent — tenant_id propagation extends to rate-limiter and quota counters), ADR-003 (parent — authenticated identity is the rate-limit subject), ADR-005 (parent — quota events feed observability + SIEM), ADR-006 (sibling — runtime namespace quotas are the lower layer; this ADR is the application-layer layer above it), ADR-007 (sibling — export pipeline rate-limited under the policy in this ADR).
- **Cross-project**: project 002 BR-001 (single codebase; sovereign deploys with overridable quotas), FR-006 (within-deployment isolation between projects/communities of interest — sovereign quotas may be per-project), NFR-A-003 parity.

## 4. Decision Drivers (Forces)

### 4.1 Technical Drivers

- **Bulkhead at the request layer (NFR-A-003)**
  - Shared cells need a per-tenant cap that fires before namespace-level resource quotas (ADR-006) trigger pod evictions.

- **Token-bucket semantics with burst allowance**
  - Requirements: NFR-P-001 (3 s p95 must hold under burst), NFR-A-003.
  - SME tenants do bursty work (a daily auth-and-edit session, then quiet); a token-bucket lets short bursts pass while smoothing sustained load.

- **AI-inference budget cap (FR-004 + Principle 17)**
  - Per-tenant monthly inference token / call cap; soft warning at 80 %, hard throttle at 100 %; bound per-tenant cost-to-serve.

- **Export rate-limit (FR-006, ADR-007)**
  - Export is right-of-portability; it cannot be denied. But repeated full-tenant exports within minutes are a self-inflicted DDoS on the export pipeline. Cap at N exports / day with documented reason.

- **Quotas must propagate tenant_id (defence in depth above ADR-001)**
  - Every rate-limit decision and every quota-counter increment carries the tenant_id; same SIEM rules detect quota anomalies as detect cross-tenant attempts.

- **Quotas applied at the API gateway (edge), not in every service**
  - Single enforcement point; uniform policy; lower implementation cost; clearer evidence trail.

- **Tier differentiation as configuration, not code**
  - Tier definitions are values in tenant configuration; new tiers (or tier tuning) deployable via GitOps (ADR-006) without code change.

- **Sovereign-mode override (project 002)**
  - Deploying authority sets their own quotas via a values overlay (per ADR-006 sovereign profile pattern); SaaS-tier defaults *not* baked into the runtime.

### 4.2 Business Drivers

- **SME free-tier cost ceiling (BR-001, Principle 1)**
  - The free tier is only viable if the worst-case per-free-tenant cost-to-serve is bounded. AI inference is the largest variable cost; it needs a hard cap.

- **Cross-subsidy break-even (BR-005)**
  - Paid tiers must justify their price by tier-differentiated quotas (more API throughput, more AI inference, more export frequency, larger storage envelope). Without tier differentiation the commercial model collapses.

- **Transparent published fair-use (BR-002)**
  - SME architects must be able to model their own cost base before committing; opaque rate-limits are an SME-hostile pattern that contradicts Principle 1.

- **G-Cloud listing (BR-004)**
  - Listings need a fair-use statement; this ADR is the centre of that statement.

- **Commercial differentiation between SME paid tier and large-enterprise tier**
  - Tier-differentiated quotas make the value of the paid tier observable to the buyer.

### 4.3 Regulatory & Compliance Drivers

- **GDS Service Standard Point 5 (Make sure everyone can use the service)**: quotas must not silently lock genuine SME users out — published thresholds, observable usage telemetry per tenant, soft warnings before hard throttle.
- **GDS Service Standard Point 14 (Operate a reliable service)**: fair-use is the operational discipline that makes the service reliable for all tenants.
- **Technology Code of Practice Point 4 (Make security integral)**: rate-limiting is a baseline denial-of-service control.
- **Cyber Essentials**: secure configuration includes denial-of-service mitigation.
- **UK GDPR fairness principle**: transparent disclosure of usage processing; data subjects (tenant users) and tenants (controllers) know what is measured.
- **Consumer Rights Act 2015 (where commercial tiers apply)**: published terms must reflect actual fair-use enforcement.

## 5. Considered Options

### Option 1: Tier-Differentiated Token-Bucket Rate-Limits at the API Gateway, AI-Inference Monthly Budget Cap, Export Rate-Limit, with a Published Fair-Use Policy (Recommended)

**Description**: A single enforcement point — the API gateway / edge layer — applies tenant-aware token-bucket rate-limits per route class (interactive, AI, export, admin). Per-tenant token-bucket parameters are derived from the tenant's tier (free / SME paid / large-enterprise / custom). Soft warnings fire at 80 % of any quota; hard throttles return HTTP 429 with `Retry-After` and a tenant-readable explanation. AI inference is metered separately with a *monthly* budget cap (calls and tokens), because inference is the dominant variable cost. Export is rate-limited per day. A *fair-use policy* is published on the pricing page and tenant portal, defining the thresholds and the tenant's right to request a temporary uplift via the admin console (FR-014). Sovereign deployments (project 002) use the same enforcement code with values-overlay configuration; the deploying authority sets their own thresholds.

**Implementation Approach**:

- **Enforcement point**: API gateway / edge ingress in front of services (per ADR-006 runtime). Single source of truth for the rate-limit decision; every backend service trusts the gateway.
- **Subjects**: tenant_id is the primary subject; user_id is a secondary subject for per-user-within-tenant fair-use (e.g., one rogue user inside a tenant can't exhaust the tenant's allotment for everyone).
- **Route classes** (initial set; tunable):
  - **Interactive** (UI + light API): high token-bucket capacity, high refill rate.
  - **Bulk read** (list / search): medium capacity, medium refill.
  - **Write / mutation**: low-medium capacity (writes are heavier).
  - **AI generation** (FR-004): metered separately with monthly budget; per-request rate-limit prevents single-tenant bursts; monthly budget bounds total cost.
  - **Export** (FR-006, ADR-007): N exports / 24h; documented reason recorded for abuse-pattern review.
  - **Admin** (FR-014, billing changes, tier change, quota uplift request): low rate; re-auth required (per ADR-003 / NFR-SEC-001).
- **Tier definitions** (initial set; refine in SOBC):

  | Class | Free SME | Paid SME | Large Enterprise | Sovereign (project 002) |
  |-------|----------|----------|------------------|-------------------------|
  | Interactive (req/min) | indicative — see SOBC | indicative — see SOBC | indicative — see SOBC | overlay-configurable |
  | AI inference (calls/mo) | hard cap | hard cap (10× free) | hard cap (configurable) | overlay-configurable |
  | AI inference (tokens/mo) | hard cap | hard cap | hard cap | overlay-configurable |
  | Export (per 24h) | 3 | 10 | 50 | overlay-configurable |
  | Storage (per tenant) | proportionate to NFR-S-002 envelope | larger | configurable | overlay |
  | API requests (per min) | indicative — see SOBC | indicative — see SOBC | indicative — see SOBC | overlay |
  | Concurrent sessions | indicative | indicative | indicative | overlay |

  *(Specific numbers deliberately not committed in this ADR — they are SOBC-level, tunable post-GA via GitOps without ADR change. The shape is the architectural commitment.)*

- **Soft warning + hard throttle**: at 80 % of monthly quota a warning is emitted to the tenant admin (in-product banner + email per INT-004); at 100 % the relevant route class returns HTTP 429 with `Retry-After` and a JSON body explaining what was exceeded and how to request uplift.
- **Tenant-uplift workflow** (admin console — FR-014): tenant admin requests temporary uplift; SLA for response (24 h business hours); operator approves with documented reason; uplift recorded in per-tenant audit log (FR-012).
- **Operator override (FR-014)**: SRE on call can grant emergency uplift; logged; reviewed in next SIEM cycle.
- **Observability (per ADR-005)**: every rate-limit decision emits an event with tenant_id, route class, decision (allow/throttle), and remaining-budget delta. SIEM rules detect:
  - sustained throttling on a tenant (operational alert — possible legitimate workload shift, candidate for uplift conversation),
  - throttling concurrent with cross-tenant attempts (security alert — possible reconnaissance),
  - AI-budget exhaustion patterns (FinOps alert — cross-subsidy review trigger).
- **Tenant-visible quota status (FR-014)**: tenant admin sees current consumption vs quota for every route class; aligns with BR-002 (transparent published pricing — current usage made visible).
- **Fair-use policy**: published page on the pricing site and tenant portal; covers what is measured, what the thresholds are, the soft-warn / hard-throttle behaviour, the uplift process, and abuse-pattern enforcement.
- **Abuse-pattern enforcement**: separate from quotas — patterns indicating adversarial behaviour (e.g., distributed credential stuffing, scraping, AI-generation as data-exfiltration) fall to NFR-SEC-006 / SIEM rules per ADR-005, not to fair-use; this ADR explicitly leaves that boundary clean.
- **Sovereign profile (project 002)**: same enforcement code; values overlay supplies the tier table; deploying authority can disable tier-differentiation entirely (single internal "tier") and configure absolute caps that match their cell capacity.

**Wardley Evolution Stage**: Commodity (token-bucket rate-limiting at the edge is a utility pattern; managed API gateways and Kubernetes ingress controllers all support it).

#### Good (Pros)

- ✅ **Single enforcement point** — uniform policy; one place to audit, one place to reason about, one place to test.
- ✅ **Tier differentiation as values, not code** — new tiers and tier tuning ship via GitOps (ADR-006) without ADR rework.
- ✅ **AI-inference budget cap = SME free-tier viability** — this is the single most important per-tenant cost-to-serve control (BR-001, BR-005, Principle 17).
- ✅ **Bulkhead above ADR-001 + ADR-006** — defence in depth at the request layer; bursts hit token-bucket before they hit namespace quotas.
- ✅ **Token-bucket allows legitimate SME bursts** — daily-session pattern works without false positives.
- ✅ **Soft warning + hard throttle is SME-friendly** — tenant gets visibility before the wall, not at it.
- ✅ **Published fair-use policy** — Principle 1 (transparency to SME), BR-002, recognisable to GDS / Cyber Essentials.
- ✅ **Sovereign-mode parity (project 002)** — values-overlay reuses the same code; no fork.
- ✅ **Export rate-limit prevents BR-007 weaponisation** — exit promise preserved without making the export pipeline a DDoS vector.
- ✅ **Rate-limit events feed observability + SIEM** (ADR-005) — abuse-pattern detection cheap and noise-free.

#### Bad (Cons)

- ❌ **Initial threshold tuning will be wrong somewhere** — first 6 months will need rate-limit tuning as real workloads land. Mitigated by quarterly review cadence in this ADR; tuning is configuration not code.
- ❌ **Token-bucket is per-instance unless coordinated via shared store** — needs a shared counter (Redis or equivalent) for cell-wide accuracy. Mitigated by a managed cache/store as part of the gateway stack.
- ❌ **Tenant-uplift workflow needs people-process** — operators must respond within SLA. Mitigated by FR-014 admin console workflow and 24h business-hours SLA.
- ❌ **Fair-use policy text is now a contract** — material changes require ARB review and 60-day tenant notice (mirrors BR-002 pricing-change cadence).
- ❌ **AI-inference budget complexity** — call-count plus token-count is two dimensions; tenants will need to understand both. Mitigated by tenant-portal dashboard showing both.

### Option 2: Quota-by-Code (Hard Coded Limits in Each Service, No Edge Gateway Layer)

**Description**: Every service implements its own quota and rate-limit logic against a shared store. No single API-gateway enforcement layer.

**Wardley Evolution Stage**: Custom-built (anti-pattern).

#### Good

- ✅ Per-service tuning possible; specific endpoints can have specific behaviour.
- ✅ No single-point-of-failure on the gateway.

#### Bad

- ❌ **Policy duplication and drift** — every new service has to reimplement; quota-aware testing every time.
- ❌ **Inconsistent tenant experience** — one service throttles before another, giving confusing 429s.
- ❌ **Harder to evidence at GDS / Cyber Essentials assessment** — no single trust boundary for the rate-limit control.
- ❌ **Higher implementation cost** — the team is small; bespoke per service is unsustainable.
- ❌ **Sovereign-profile fragmentation** — each service must support overlay configuration; multiplicative complexity.

### Option 3: Do Nothing — Rely on Cell-Level Namespace Quotas Only (ADR-006) and Application-Layer Tenant_id (ADR-001)

**Description**: Trust ADR-001 + ADR-006 to handle isolation and bulkhead; no application-layer per-tenant rate-limit beyond what Kubernetes namespace quotas provide.

**Wardley Evolution Stage**: N/A.

#### Good

- ✅ Zero immediate engineering cost.

#### Bad

- ❌ **Namespace quotas fire at *cell* level, not at *per-tenant* level within a cell** — one tenant can saturate the cell-shared resources (compute, AI-inference quota with the upstream provider, export pipeline back-pressure) while still being below the namespace cap.
- ❌ **AI-inference cost-to-serve unbounded per tenant** — directly breaks BR-001 free-tier viability and BR-005 cross-subsidy economics. **This alone disqualifies the option.**
- ❌ **Export pipeline cannot be defended from repeated full-tenant exports** — BR-007 exit right becomes a self-inflicted DDoS vector.
- ❌ **No tier differentiation** — FR-011 commercial tiers have no technical surface to ride on.
- ❌ **GDS Point 5 + 14 weakened** — no published fair-use; tenants experience *unpredictable* failure modes from cell saturation.
- ❌ **Sovereign-mode parity broken (Principle 21)** — sovereign deploying authorities have no way to bound per-project consumption inside their deployment.

**Verdict**: Not viable. Namespace quotas are necessary but not sufficient.

## 6. Decision Outcome

### 6.1 Chosen Option

**Option 1 — Tier-differentiated token-bucket rate-limits at the API gateway + AI-inference monthly budget cap + export rate-limit + published fair-use policy. Sovereign deployments configure thresholds via values overlay (project 002).**

### 6.2 Y-Statement

> **In the context of** running ArcKit as a Service as a multi-tenant SaaS in shared cells (ADR-001 / ADR-006), where AI inference is the dominant variable cost-to-serve, where the SME free tier (BR-001) and the cross-subsidy commercial model (BR-005) both require predictable per-tenant cost ceilings, and where the same controls must be configurable by sovereign deploying authorities (project 002),
> **facing** the conflict between SME-friendly burst tolerance (legitimate daily-session bursts must not be throttled) and protecting shared cells, AI-inference cost, and the export pipeline from a single noisy or abusive tenant,
> **we decided for** tier-differentiated token-bucket rate-limits applied at the API gateway, an AI-inference monthly budget cap (calls + tokens), an export rate-limit, soft warnings at 80 % with hard throttle at 100 %, an admin-console uplift workflow, a published fair-use policy, and the same enforcement code reused with sovereign-overlay configuration in project 002,
> **to achieve** request-layer bulkhead above ADR-001 and ADR-006, predictable per-tenant cost-to-serve preserving SME free-tier viability, transparent commercial tier differentiation, BR-007 export rights protected from weaponisation, and one enforcement code-path across SaaS and sovereign,
> **accepting** that initial thresholds will need post-GA tuning, that the gateway needs a coordinated rate-limit store, that AI-budget metering carries two dimensions (calls + tokens), and that the fair-use policy text now functions as a tenant-facing contract requiring 60-day change notice.

### 6.3 Justification

1. **AI-inference budget cap is the single most important cost control** — without it, BR-001 free-tier viability collapses on the first abusive tenant. Option 3 is disqualified by this fact alone.
2. **Single enforcement point at the edge** is the only sustainable shape for a small team — Option 2's per-service implementation is multiplicative engineering cost and a drift surface the platform cannot afford.
3. **Tier-differentiation as configuration** lets FR-011 commercial tiers be tuned post-GA without ADR rework — important because real workload data after GA will move thresholds.
4. **Defence in depth above ADR-001 and ADR-006** — token-bucket fires before namespace-level resource quotas; the cell stays healthy for everyone.
5. **Soft-warn + hard-throttle + uplift workflow** is the SME-respectful pattern — Principle 1 and BR-002 demand transparency rather than silent failure.
6. **Sovereign-overlay parity (project 002)** — the same enforcement code, with deploying authority controlling thresholds, satisfies Principle 21 single-codebase commitment.
7. **Export rate-limit (ADR-007 sibling)** — protects BR-007 right by making the pipeline operationally robust against repeated bursts.

**Stakeholder consensus**: Service Owner + FinOps Lead + (PENDING) SRE Lead aligned on AI-inference budget cap as the deciding force. DPO confirmed that publishing the fair-use policy satisfies the UK GDPR fairness principle for usage-data processing. Project 002 liaison confirmed values-overlay configurability is sufficient.

**Dissenting view to record**: A view exists that the free tier should not have an AI-inference cap at all, to maximise SME adoption (BR-008). Rejected — uncapped AI inference cannot be defended against the cross-subsidy unit economics in BR-005; the alternative is a free tier without AI, which is worse for SME utility.

**Risk appetite**: Per-tenant cost-to-serve dominance over BR-005; bulkhead protection above ADR-001 / ADR-006; AI-inference cap is non-negotiable; threshold tuning is operational and acceptable.

### 7.2 Negative Consequences (Accepted Trade-offs)

- ❌ **Initial thresholds will need post-GA tuning** — quarterly review for first year accepted as part of operational discipline.

- ❌ **Coordinated rate-limit store needed** — Redis or managed cache shared across gateway pods.
  - *Mitigation*: managed cache as part of cell deployment; same UK region per ADR-002.
- ❌ **AI-budget metering has two dimensions (calls + tokens)** — UI must surface both clearly.
  - *Mitigation*: tenant-portal dashboard; documented in fair-use policy.
- ❌ **Fair-use policy is a tenant-facing contract** — material changes require 60-day notice (parity with BR-002 pricing).
  - *Mitigation*: change cadence ≤ 1/year; ARB review before any material change.
- ❌ **Tenant-uplift workflow needs people-process** — operator response within 24 h business hours.

```mermaid
flowchart TD
  R[Request arrives at gateway] --> AUTH{Authenticated?\n(per ADR-003)}

  CLASS --> BUCKET[Decrement token bucket\n(tenant_id, route_class)\nshared store]
  BUCKET --> AVAIL{Tokens available?}
  AVAIL -- No --> H429[HTTP 429\nRetry-After + reason]
  AVAIL -- Yes --> AICHK{AI route?\n+budget remaining?}
  AICHK -- No (AI exhausted) --> H429
  AICHK -- N/A or remaining --> SOFT{Approaching\n80% threshold?}
  SOFT -- Yes --> WARN[Emit soft-warn event\nin-product banner + email\n(INT-004)]
  SOFT -- No --> PASS[Forward to backend]
  WARN --> PASS
  PASS --> EVENT[Emit quota event\nto ADR-005 pipeline\nfor SIEM + dashboards]
  H429 --> EVENT
```
