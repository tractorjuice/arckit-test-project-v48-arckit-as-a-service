## 2. Context and Problem Statement

ArcKit's AI-assisted generation (FR-004, UC-2) is core to the value proposition: it is what makes a free SME tier viable by removing hours of manual drafting from each artefact. The AI integration must:

- **Resolve a tenant_id at every call** (ADR-001; NFR-SEC-002) — including downstream to the AI endpoint.
- **Keep tenant data UK-resident** (Principle 7; NFR-C-001) — provider region matters as much for AI as for storage.
- **Avoid training on tenant data** by default — contractual no-train terms required; tenant opt-in to share data for model improvement only.
- **Produce auditable generation events** (Principle 13 AI ethics; NFR-C-002 audit logging) — every generation logged with model, prompt-shape, and tenant context.
- **Remain pluggable** so that:
  - The provider can be swapped without architectural change (Principle 4; FR-004 acceptance criteria).
  - Project 002 can plug in a customer-controlled / on-prem model (project 002 FR-004; INT-005).
  - SME tenants can opt for a free-tier model (cost-controlled) and enterprise tenants can opt for a higher-capability model (paid tier).
- **Fail safely** — generation failure must not block manual artefact authoring (FR-003).
- **Meet the UK Government AI Playbook** — transparency, human oversight, bias mitigation, ATRS where relevant.

This ADR records the AI provider abstraction and the default-model strategy.

## 4. Considered Options

### Option A — Provider-Agnostic Adaptor with Tier-Based Default Model Selection (Two Providers from Day One) **(Recommended)**

**Description**: All AI calls go through a single internal adaptor (`AIAdaptor`). The adaptor exposes a small interface (`generate`, `generateStream`, `embed`) and is implemented for at least two providers from day one (e.g., Anthropic Claude and OpenAI; or Anthropic Claude and AWS Bedrock; specific selection via `/arckit:research`). Tenant tier drives default model selection: free tier uses an efficient low-cost model; paid tiers may opt into a higher-capability model. Every call carries tenant_id, is rate-limited and budget-capped per tenant, is logged with model name, token count, latency, and outcome, and is annotated in the generated artefact with model identity and timestamp (transparency requirement).

**Implementation Approach**:

- **Adaptor interface** (`AIAdaptor`): `generate(prompt, tenant_id, model, options)`, `generateStream(...)`, `embed(text, tenant_id, model)`. Provider-specific code lives in `providers/` only.
- **Tenant tier → default model** mapping in tenant config (FR-002).
- **Per-tenant budget ceiling** (monthly $ or token cap) enforced before call; circuit-breaker stops calls when exhausted; tenant notified.
- **Per-tenant rate limit** (NFR-A-003 bulkhead) — noisy tenant cannot exhaust shared inference quota.
- **Prompt logging**: prompt-shape (template id + variable schema) logged; full prompt content **not** logged by default to avoid creating a large secondary copy of tenant content; full prompt may be retained for ≤ 7 days behind operator opt-in for incident investigation.
- **Generation provenance**: every artefact section produced by AI carries machine-readable metadata (model, version, timestamp, prompt template id) — supports transparency (AI Playbook) and ATRS evidence.
- **No training on customer data** is contractual default with both providers; a tenant may opt-in to share data for model improvement (off by default; tenant-admin only; logged).
- **Sovereign reuse (project 002)**: the same `AIAdaptor` interface is implemented for an on-prem or customer-approved AI endpoint; if no AI provider is approved, the AI feature degrades gracefully (FR-003 manual authoring still works; AI generation marked unavailable).

**Wardley Evolution Stage**: Custom-built (the abstraction); Product (the LLMs themselves; commodity is not yet appropriate as model differentiation is significant).

**Pros (Good)**:

- ✅ **Pluggable**: provider swap is a config and adaptor implementation, not an architectural change.
- ✅ **Tier-aware cost discipline**: free tier uses efficient model; paid tier may opt up.
- ✅ **Per-tenant budget caps** prevent any one tenant from depleting shared inference cost.
- ✅ **Tenant_id propagation downstream** is a first-class requirement, covered by CI isolation test on the AI surface.
- ✅ **Sovereign-reusable** via the same adaptor.
- ✅ **AI Playbook transparency** baked in via generation provenance.

**Cons (Bad)**:

- ❌ **Two-provider integration cost** from day one (vs single provider); justified as the only credible mitigation against single-provider lock-in.
- ❌ **Provider DPA review burden** — annual DPA review for each provider; mitigated by adaptor isolating change blast radius.
- ❌ **Model behaviour drift** (provider model updates) requires regression test on artefact templates; mitigated by golden-prompt suite in CI.

**Cost Analysis**:

| Component | CAPEX | OPEX (annual) | TCO 3-year |
|-----------|-------|---------------|-----------|
| Adaptor + two provider integrations | TBD (eng) | low (maintenance) | low |
| Per-tenant inference (free tier, modelled) | n/a | low (efficient model) | depends on adoption |
| Per-tenant inference (paid tier) | n/a | medium (higher-capability) | recoverable in pricing |
| Golden-prompt regression infrastructure | TBD | low | low |

**GDS Service Standard Impact**: Points 5 (everyone can use), 6 (multidisciplinary — AI Ethics involved), 9 (security — tenant_id propagation), 12 (open standards — provider-agnostic).

### Option B — Single-Provider Lock-In

**Description**: One AI provider, called directly throughout the codebase.

**Cons**:

- ❌ Direct conflict with FR-004 acceptance criteria (provider swappable in a sprint).
- ❌ Direct conflict with Principle 4 (open standards / portability).
- ❌ Provider-pricing or DPA-term change is a service-impacting event with no escape valve.

**Verdict**: Not selected.

---

### Option C — Self-Hosted Open-Weight Model Only

**Description**: Vendor hosts an open-weight model (e.g., Llama-class) on UK GPU infrastructure; no third-party AI provider.

**Pros**:

- ✅ No third-party AI sub-processor.
- ✅ Strongest data-residency narrative.

**Cons**:

- ❌ **Capability gap** at current open-weight quality vs frontier proprietary models for the artefact-generation workload.
- ❌ **GPU cost** (capex / opex) catastrophic for SME free tier.
- ❌ **Operational maturity required** — model serving, scaling, observability — outside small team's wheelhouse.
- ❌ Project 002 already addresses the truly sovereign use case; replicating it on the SaaS doubles cost without addressing a stakeholder need.

**Verdict**: Not selected for SaaS. Available as one of the providers behind the adaptor on opt-in for tenants who demand it (cost recovery via paid tier).

---

### Option D — No AI Generation

**Description**: Drop FR-004; manual authoring only.

**Cons**:

- ❌ Removes the differentiation that makes the SME free tier economically rational.
- ❌ Direct conflict with FR-004, UC-2.

**Verdict**: Not viable.

## 5. Decision Outcome

### Chosen Option

**Option A — Provider-agnostic adaptor with at least two production-tested providers from day one; tier-based default model selection; per-tenant budget caps and rate limits; generation provenance recorded; no-train contractual default; sovereign-reusable adaptor.**

### Y-Statement

> In the context of delivering AI-assisted artefact generation on a UK-resident multi-tenant SaaS that must remain SME-affordable, transparent under the UK Government AI Playbook, and portable to a sovereign deployment route,
> facing the conflict between provider-capability differentiation and the architectural and commercial risk of provider lock-in,
> we decided for **a provider-agnostic adaptor with two production-tested providers from day one, tier-based default-model selection, per-tenant budget caps, generation provenance, and no-training contractual defaults**,
> to achieve **portable AI capability, cost-disciplined SME free tier, AI Playbook transparency, and sovereign reuse via the same adaptor**,
> accepting **two-provider integration cost, ongoing DPA review burden, and a regression-test investment to manage model-behaviour drift**.
