## 3. Context and Problem Statement

### 3.1 Problem Description

ArcKit's AI-assisted artefact generation (FR-004, UC-2) is core to the value proposition: it is what makes a free SME tier viable by removing hours of manual drafting from each artefact. The integration must keep tenant data UK-resident, never train on tenant content by default, propagate `tenant_id` to every provider call, produce auditable generation events with provenance metadata, remain pluggable so the provider can be swapped without architectural change, fail safely (manual authoring per FR-003 always works), and meet the UK Government AI Playbook on transparency, oversight, and accountability.

**Problem statement as a question**: How do we deliver AI-assisted generation that is portable across providers, cost-disciplined per tenant, auditable, sovereign-reusable, and AI-Playbook-compliant?

### 3.2 Why This Decision Is Needed

- **Business context**: BR-001 (free tier viability depends on efficient default model), BR-005 (paid tiers may opt up), BR-006 (UK Government policy evidence — AI Playbook).
- **Technical context**: FR-004 (AI generation), UC-2 (AI-assisted drafting), INT-005 (AI provider integration), NFR-P-002 (generation latency), NFR-A-003 (graceful degradation), NFR-SEC-002 (tenant isolation extends to AI), NFR-C-001 (UK GDPR), NFR-I-001 (open-standard interfaces).
- **Regulatory context**: UK Government AI Playbook; ICO AI guidance; Algorithmic Transparency Recording Standard (ATRS).

### 3.3 Supporting Links

## 5. Considered Options

### Option 1: Provider-Agnostic Adaptor with Tier-Based Default Model (Two Providers from Day One) (Recommended)

**Description**: All AI calls go through a single internal `AIAdaptor` interface (`generate`, `generateStream`, `embed`). The adaptor is implemented for at least two providers from day one (specific selection via `/arckit:research`). Tenant tier drives default model selection: free tier uses an efficient low-cost model; paid tiers may opt into higher-capability models. Every call carries `tenant_id`, is rate-limited and budget-capped per tenant, is logged with model name, token count, latency, outcome, and is annotated in the generated artefact with model identity and timestamp (transparency requirement).

**Implementation approach**:

- **Adaptor interface**: `AIAdaptor.generate(prompt, tenant_id, model, options)`, `generateStream`, `embed`. Provider-specific code lives in `providers/` only.
- **Tenant tier → default model** mapping in tenant config (FR-002).
- **Per-tenant budget ceiling** (monthly tokens or $) enforced before call; circuit-breaker stops calls when exhausted; tenant notified.
- **Per-tenant rate limit** (NFR-A-003 bulkhead) — noisy tenant cannot exhaust shared inference quota.
- **Prompt logging**: prompt-shape (template id + variable schema) logged; full prompt content **not** logged by default; full prompt may be retained for ≤ 7 days behind operator opt-in for incident investigation.
- **Generation provenance**: every artefact section produced by AI carries machine-readable metadata (model, version, timestamp, prompt template id) — supports transparency (AI Playbook) and ATRS evidence.
- **No training on customer data** is contractual default with both providers; tenant may opt-in to share data for model improvement (off by default; tenant-admin only; logged).
- **Sovereign reuse (project 002)**: same `AIAdaptor` interface implemented for on-prem or customer-approved AI endpoint; AI feature degrades gracefully if no provider configured (FR-003 manual authoring still works).

**Wardley Evolution Stage**: Custom-built (the abstraction); Product (the LLMs themselves).

#### Good (Pros)

- ✅ **Pluggable**: provider swap is config + adaptor implementation, not architectural change.
- ✅ **Tier-aware cost discipline**: free tier uses efficient model; paid tier may opt up.
- ✅ **Per-tenant budget caps** prevent any one tenant from depleting shared inference cost.
- ✅ **Tenant_id propagation downstream** is first-class; covered by CI isolation test on the AI surface.
- ✅ **Sovereign-reusable** via the same adaptor.
- ✅ **AI Playbook transparency** baked in via generation provenance.

#### Bad (Cons)

- ❌ **Two-provider integration cost** from day one (vs single provider); justified as the only credible mitigation against single-provider lock-in.
- ❌ **Provider DPA review burden** — annual DPA review for each provider; mitigated by adaptor isolating change blast radius.
- ❌ **Model behaviour drift** (provider model updates) requires regression test on artefact templates; mitigated by golden-prompt suite in CI.

#### Cost Analysis

- **CAPEX**: Adaptor + two provider implementations + golden-prompt regression infrastructure.
- **OPEX**: Per-tenant inference cost (low at free-tier model, medium at paid-tier model); regression suite execution.
- **TCO (3-year)**: Recoverable via paid tiers; preserves free tier viability.

## 6. Decision Outcome

### 6.1 Chosen Option

**"Option 1: Provider-Agnostic Adaptor with Tier-Based Default Model and Two Production-Tested Providers from Day One"**

### 6.2 Y-Statement

> **In the context of** delivering AI-assisted artefact generation on a UK-resident multi-tenant SaaS that must remain SME-affordable, transparent under the UK Government AI Playbook, and portable to a sovereign deployment route,
> **facing** the conflict between provider-capability differentiation and the architectural and commercial risk of provider lock-in,
> **we decided for** a provider-agnostic adaptor with two production-tested providers from day one, tier-based default-model selection, per-tenant budget caps, generation provenance, and no-training contractual defaults,
> **to achieve** portable AI capability, cost-disciplined SME free tier, AI Playbook transparency, and sovereign reuse via the same adaptor,
> **accepting** two-provider integration cost, ongoing DPA review burden, and a regression-test investment to manage model-behaviour drift.

### 6.3 Justification

1. **Pluggability is non-negotiable**: FR-004 acceptance criteria + Principle 4 + project 002 reuse all demand it.
2. **Tier-aware cost discipline**: only path to a free tier that doesn't bankrupt the cross-subsidy model (BR-005).
3. **Sovereign reuse via same adaptor**: the most direct way to satisfy Principle 21 for the AI capability.
4. **Transparency baked in**: provenance metadata is the AI Playbook evidence asset.
5. **Two providers as risk mitigation**: any provider DPA/pricing/capability shock has a real escape valve.

**Stakeholder consensus**: Service Owner, DPO, Vendor Security Lead aligned. AI Ethics reviewer (when appointed) to confirm provenance schema.

**Risk appetite**: Two-provider integration cost accepted as the price of optionality; lock-in risk has higher long-run cost.

## 7. Consequences

### 7.1 Positive Consequences

- ✅ AI provider can be swapped without application change.
- ✅ SME free tier viable via efficient default model + budget caps.
- ✅ AI Playbook transparency baked in (generation provenance, audit log).
- ✅ Sovereign reuse via same adaptor; AI degrades gracefully when no provider configured.

**Measurable outcomes**:

- Provider-specific call sites in app code: 0 (lint-enforced) — Principle 4; FR-004.
- AI calls without tenant_id: 0 (CI-tested) — ADR-001.
- Per-tenant monthly inference budget enforced: 100 % (circuit-breaker) — NFR-A-003.
- Generation events with provenance metadata: 100 % — AI Playbook.
- Provider DPA reviewed in last 12 months: 100 % — NFR-C-001.
- Golden-prompt regression suite green per release: 100 %.

### 7.2 Negative Consequences (Accepted Trade-offs)

- ❌ Two-provider integration cost from day one.
- ❌ Annual DPA review for each provider.
- ❌ Golden-prompt regression suite must be maintained as templates evolve.

**Mitigation strategies**:

- **Two-provider cost**: justified by lock-in risk avoidance and AI Playbook portability evidence.
- **DPA review**: scheduled quarterly; alternate provider on standby.
- **Drift management**: golden-prompt regression suite green-required per release.

### 7.3 Neutral Consequences

- 🔄 Per-tenant `monthly_ai_budget` exposed in tenant admin UI (FR-002, FR-014).
- 🔄 Tenant opt-in for "share data for model improvement" off by default; documented in DPIA.
- 🔄 Generation provenance metadata schema versioned in OpenAPI (NFR-I-001).
