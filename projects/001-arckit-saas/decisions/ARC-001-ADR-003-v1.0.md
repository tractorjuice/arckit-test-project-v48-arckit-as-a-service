## 2. Context and Problem Statement

ArcKit must support three distinct identity populations:

1. **SME tenant users** (UC-1 self-service onboarding) — typically 1–10 users per tenant; no enterprise IdP; need fast onboarding and no per-tenant integration cost.
2. **Enterprise tenant users** — buying authorities and large suppliers (SD-1 to SD-5; SD-6) — already operate an enterprise IdP (Entra ID, Okta, Ping, etc.) and require federated SSO with their existing identity provider.
3. **Vendor service operators** (FR-014 admin console) — small population, must use phishing-resistant MFA and step-up authentication for sensitive operations.

Constraints:

- **Authentication is non-negotiable** (Principle 5; NFR-SEC-001): MFA mandatory for write access; phishing-resistant for service operators.
- **Tenant isolation must be identity-anchored** (ADR-001; NFR-SEC-002): the tenant_id is resolved from identity claims at every request boundary.
- **UK Government users may use GOV.UK One Login** where the buying authority adopts it; this is an accelerating path that the architecture should not obstruct.
- **SME affordability** (Principle 1): per-tenant identity cost must round to zero.
- **Project 002 sovereign deployments use customer IdP exclusively** (project 002 FR-007): the SaaS architecture must not assume vendor IdP at the **shape** layer — the same code must run with no vendor IdP at all.

This ADR records the chosen identity strategy.

## 4. Considered Options

### Option A — Vendor-Hosted IdP (OIDC) for SMEs + Self-Service Federation (OIDC + SAML 2.0) for Enterprise/Government Tenants + Hardware-Key MFA for Service Operators **(Recommended)**

**Description**: Use a managed identity provider (e.g., AWS Cognito, Auth0, WorkOS, Microsoft Entra External ID, Keycloak managed) for SME tenants who do not bring their own IdP. The same product surface offers self-service SAML 2.0 / OIDC federation configuration for enterprise tenants and government buying authorities. Service operators authenticate via a separate, hardened admin IdP with mandatory hardware-key WebAuthn. All identity flows resolve a tenant_id claim that is propagated through the request pipeline per ADR-001.

**Implementation Approach**:

- **SME tenants**: vendor-hosted IdP; user signup with email verification; MFA required for write access (TOTP free, WebAuthn supported).
- **Enterprise tenants**: tenant admin configures OIDC or SAML 2.0 federation in self-service UI (FR-007); domain-claim verification (DNS TXT) prevents tenant-domain hijack.
- **GOV.UK One Login**: configured as an external OIDC IdP at the per-tenant level — a buying authority can route their users via One Login; not exclusive (the same tenant may also have direct accounts).
- **Service operators (FR-014)**: separate admin IdP; mandatory hardware-key (WebAuthn / FIDO2) MFA; step-up authentication required for tenant-data-impacting operations; session lifetime ≤ 8 hours.
- **Claim mapping** documented and version-controlled; tenant_id claim required and validated at every gateway boundary; default-deny if absent.
- **Project 002 reuse**: the IdP integration sits behind a generic OIDC / SAML adaptor; project 002 plugs in customer IdP (FR-007 in project 002) with no application-level changes.

**Wardley Evolution Stage**: Commodity / utility — managed IdP and OIDC/SAML federation are well-understood patterns.

**Pros (Good)**:

- ✅ **SME tenants onboarded with zero per-tenant integration cost** — preserves UC-1 and SME affordability.
- ✅ **Enterprise tenants federate without vendor engineering** — FR-007 self-service.
- ✅ **GOV.UK One Login supported as an external IdP** — BR-006 evidence; SD-13 alignment.
- ✅ **Phishing-resistant MFA for operators** — addresses the highest-blast-radius accounts (SD-10, SD-3).
- ✅ **Generic OIDC/SAML adaptor portable to project 002** — Principle 21 satisfied.
- ✅ **Claim-based tenant_id resolution** integrates cleanly with ADR-001 isolation model.

**Cons (Bad)**:

- ❌ **Vendor IdP becomes a sub-processor** — must be UK-resident or covered by IDTA / Adequacy (DPO inventory; ADR-002).
- ❌ **Federation-onboarding bugs are tenant-impacting** — mitigated by domain verification and integration test harness for major IdPs.
- ❌ **Token-lifetime / refresh design must be airtight** — long-lived tokens are the worst case in a multi-tenant system. Mitigated by short access-token TTL (≤15 min) + refresh tokens; refresh token rotation with reuse detection.

**Cost Analysis**:

| Managed IdP for SME tenants | TBD | low (per-MAU pricing typical) | low |
| Self-service federation UI | TBD (eng) | low | low |
| Admin IdP for service operators | TBD | low | low |
| Hardware keys for operators (issued) | low (per operator) | n/a | low |

**GDS Service Standard Impact**: Points 5 (accessibility — self-service onboarding), 9 (security — strong auth evidenced), 10 (success metrics — auth flow telemetry).

### Option B — Build Own IdP

**Description**: Vendor builds and operates its own credential store, MFA, and federation engine.

**Pros**:

- ✅ Maximum control.

**Cons**:

- ❌ **Identity is not a differentiating capability** — building it duplicates commodity work (Wardley anti-pattern).
- ❌ **Highest-risk surface** to take in-house at small team scale (SD-9, SD-10).
- ❌ **Slower path to phishing-resistant MFA** and to enterprise federation completeness.

**Verdict**: Not selected.

---

### Option C — Federated-Only (Require Tenant IdP for Every Tenant)

**Description**: Every tenant must bring their own IdP; no vendor-hosted identity.

**Pros**:

- ✅ Vendor never holds credentials.

**Cons**:

- ❌ **Catastrophic for SME tenants** who do not have an IdP (UC-1 self-service onboarding broken). Direct conflict with Principle 1 / BR-001.
- ❌ **Onboarding latency** for every tenant is the IdP-integration latency.

**Verdict**: Not selected.

---

### Option D — Single Government IdP Only (e.g., GOV.UK One Login Exclusively)

**Description**: All users must authenticate via GOV.UK One Login.

**Pros**:

- ✅ Single trust anchor; UK Government identity narrative.

**Cons**:

- ❌ One Login does not currently address the SME-supplier population at the level required.
- ❌ Excludes large enterprise tenants who federate from Entra / Okta.
- ❌ Concentration on a single external dependency.

**Verdict**: Not selected; supported as one of several federation options (Option A).

## 5. Decision Outcome

### Chosen Option

**Option A — Vendor-hosted OIDC IdP for SME tenants, self-service OIDC/SAML federation for enterprise/government tenants (including GOV.UK One Login as an external IdP option), separate hardened admin IdP with mandatory hardware-key WebAuthn for service operators.**

### Y-Statement

> In the context of supporting three distinct identity populations across an SME-affordable multi-tenant SaaS that must also serve government buying authorities and remain portable to a sovereign deployment route,
> facing the conflict between SME zero-friction onboarding and enterprise federation completeness — and the asymmetric blast radius of the service-operator population,
> we decided for **a vendor-hosted IdP for SMEs, self-service OIDC/SAML federation for enterprise/government (including GOV.UK One Login as an external IdP option), and a separate hardened admin IdP with mandatory hardware-key MFA for service operators**, all behind a generic OIDC/SAML adaptor that project 002 reuses,
> to achieve **fast SME onboarding, federation completeness without per-tenant engineering, AAL3-equivalent operator authentication, and a single identity-shape that the sovereign route can plug a customer IdP into**,
> accepting **vendor IdP as a sub-processor (DPO inventory), federation-onboarding test surface, and short access-token TTL with refresh-rotation discipline**.

## 6. Consequences

### Positive

- Self-service SME onboarding remains seconds-to-minutes (UC-1).
- Enterprise federation requires zero per-tenant engineering; supports BR-005 cross-subsidy economics.
- Hardware-key operator MFA closes the most asymmetric blast-radius account category.
- Claim-based tenant_id resolution integrates ADR-001 without bespoke per-IdP code.

**Measurable Outcomes**:

| Metric | Target | Source |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Service-operator accounts on hardware-key MFA | 100% | NFR-SEC-001 |
| Tenant-write actions without MFA | 0 | NFR-SEC-001 |
| Federation-onboarding self-service completion (without vendor engineering) | ≥ 95% | FR-007 |
| Tenant_id claim missing → request denied | 100% (CI-tested) | ADR-001 |

### Negative (Accepted)

- Sub-processor inventory expands by one (vendor IdP).
- Federation onboarding test surface is non-trivial — IdP-specific quirks (Entra, Okta, Ping, ADFS, Keycloak, One Login) tested in CI integration harness.
- Refresh-token reuse detection must be implemented carefully (a known foot-gun).

### Neutral

- Per-tenant federation status visible to tenant admin (FR-007).
- Token-lifetime policy: access ≤ 15 min, refresh ≤ 24 h (extendable on tenant policy), reuse detection terminates session family.
- Operator session lifetime ≤ 8 h; step-up required for tenant-data operations.

### Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation | Owner |
|------|------------|--------|------------|-------|
| Vendor IdP outage | LOW | HIGH | SLA review; cache last-known-good identity briefly; status page (FR-009) | SRE |
| Federation misconfiguration leaks tenant access | MEDIUM | HIGH | Domain verification (DNS TXT); per-tenant test harness; default-deny on missing claim | Security Lead |
| Refresh-token theft / replay | MEDIUM | HIGH | Rotation + reuse detection; bind refresh to client + IP class | Security Lead |
| Operator account compromise | LOW | CRITICAL | Hardware-key MFA; step-up; session limits; audit log alerts | Security Lead |
| Vendor IdP DPA term changes | LOW | MEDIUM | Annual DPA review; alternative IdP options pre-evaluated | DPO |
